Nagpur


Pincode: 440001
Nagpur is a district located in the Vidarbha region of Maharashtra.
Known as the "Orange City" due to its thriving orange cultivation, Nagpur is also a major commercial and political center in central India.
The district is known for its cultural diversity, historical landmarks, and modern infrastructure, making it a significant hub for trade, education, and tourism.

List of Popular Tourist Places in Nagpur:

  • Deekshabhoomi: A sacred monument and Buddhist stupa, known for its historical significance and annual pilgrimage.
  • Dragon Palace Temple: A beautiful Buddhist temple with intricate architecture and serene surroundings.
  • Zero Mile Stone: A landmark indicating the geographical center of India, with historical significance.
  • Ambazari Lake: A picturesque lake surrounded by gardens, offering boating facilities and a tranquil environment.
  • Sevagram: A historic village associated with Mahatma Gandhi's ashram and the Quit India Movement.
  • Raman Science Centre: A science museum and planetarium offering interactive exhibits and educational programs.
  • Maharajbagh Zoo: A zoological park housing a variety of wildlife species and botanical gardens.
  • Sitabuldi Fort: A historic fort offering panoramic views of Nagpur city and its surroundings.
  • Nagzira Wildlife Sanctuary: A wildlife sanctuary known for its rich biodiversity, including tigers, leopards, and bird species.
  • Futala Lake: A popular hangout spot with boating facilities, musical fountain shows, and food stalls.
