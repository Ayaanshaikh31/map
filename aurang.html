Aurangabad


Pincode: 431001
Aurangabad is a historic city located in the northern part of Maharashtra.
Known for its rich cultural heritage and architectural marvels, Aurangabad is home to several UNESCO World Heritage Sites.
The city is also famous for its silk and cotton textiles, as well as for being a major industrial and tourism hub in the region.

List of Popular Tourist Places in Aurangabad:

  • Ajanta Caves: A UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring ancient Buddhist rock-cut caves with intricate sculptures and paintings.
  • Ellora Caves: Another UNESCO World Heritage Site showcasing rock-cut temples and monasteries of Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain faiths.
  • Bibi Ka Maqbara: A tomb built in the memory of Aurangzeb's wife, often referred to as the "Mini Taj" due to its resemblance to the Taj Mahal.
  • Daulatabad Fort: A majestic fort with a complex defense system and historic significance, offering panoramic views of Aurangabad.
  • Panchakki (Water Mill): An ancient water mill powered by a series of water channels, known for its engineering marvel and historical importance.
  • Aurangabad Caves: Buddhist rock-cut caves dating back to the 6th and 7th centuries, featuring intricate sculptures and ancient architecture.
  • Siddharth Garden and Zoo: A sprawling garden with diverse plant species, along with a zoo housing various animals and birds.
  • Ghrishneshwar Temple: An ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva, one of the 12 Jyotirlingas in India, known for its architectural beauty.
  • Soneri Mahal: A historic palace adorned with gold paintings and intricate woodwork, reflecting the grandeur of medieval times.
  • Khuldabad: A historical town known for its Sufi shrines, including the tomb of Mughal Emperor Aurangzeb and the famous Chand Minar.
